Unlocking Secrets of Thune: A Journey of Discovery and Destiny

With the opening of the Drammensbanen in 1872, Skøyen became an attractive place for the construction of industrial activities.

A short distance to the center and port, and logistics routes with the Hoffselva and Frognerelva, together with the railway, created good conditions for extensive industrial production, which was dominant in the area well into the 20th century.

Skabo, which produced railway material, was the first to establish its business on Skøyen as early as 1869. Frognerkilens Fabrik followed in 1873, and was soon followed by several others, including Eureka and Thune. A versatile flora of smaller businesses also established themselves in the surrounding areas.

Unlocking Secrets of Thune: A Journey of Discovery and Destiny

Thune has, since Thune-Eureka moved out in 1974-75, been characterized by unplanned coincidences. After 75 years of energetic, continuous and pragmatic development, Thune became the place where there was cheap warehouse space.

Today's Thune is centrally located in one of Oslo's densest urban areas, where continuous development has taken place over the past many years. Thune lies in a kind of Sleeping Beauty, secluded in the middle of it all, a place where beautiful and pragmatic 1900s industrial heritage meets the 1970s' loose prioritization of motoring. Here are a number of immediate qualities, which can be evoked and developed to become a major asset for Skøyen and a new urban destination for the city of Oslo.

Thune's Future Unveiled: A Glimpse into Tomorrow's Wonders

It is a political goal for Skøyen to be a pioneering district for climate and environment. The purpose of the planning work is to ensure the future-oriented and comprehensive development of Skøyen as an extension of the inner city and the fjord city.

The area regulation enables urban development with many new homes and workplaces, environmentally friendly transport solutions, strengthening of blue-green structures and a new urban connection to the fjord. Skøyen will become a multifunctional 24-hour city that meets the fjord.
